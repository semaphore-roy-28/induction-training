# Activities

This folder is intended for activity prompts, instructional design exercises, and examples that faculty can work on during the creation phase of the daily session.

## Activity Types

- self-study synthesis tasks
- syllabus redesign prompts
- lab activity design challenges
- classroom discussion planning
- AI-resilient assessment design

## First Activity: Foundations of Education and Learning

This opening activity asks new faculty to reflect on four foundational questions:

- What is education?
- What is learning?
- What is teaching?
- What is your philosophy of learning?

### Purpose

The goal is not to produce a polished definition immediately. The goal is to help faculty surface their assumptions, examine them critically, and begin articulating a personal educational stance.

### Suggested Flow

1. Write a short response to each question.
2. Identify the assumptions behind each response.
3. Compare the ideas with how they would want students to learn.
4. Draft a concise philosophy of learning statement.

### How the CS Ed Expert Should Facilitate It

The CS Ed Expert should use Socratic reasoning throughout this activity. Instead of supplying answers, the facilitator should ask probing questions such as:

- When you say education, what are you trying to cultivate in a learner?
- How is learning different from memorization or performance?
- What do you believe students should be able to do, not just know?
- What experiences make learning feel meaningful or transformative to you?

The aim is to help the faculty member refine their own thinking rather than adopt a prewritten philosophy.

## Suggested Workflow

1. Choose a topic or teaching problem.
2. Create or revise an artifact.
3. Ask the evaluative agent for feedback.
4. Refine the output before the quiz phase.
